Poetry

Walking the Rows

Carol Prejean Zippert

They walked the rows
bending in rhythm to
a little chopping here,
a little picking there,
breaking backs to break crops
to break cycles.

They walked the rows
kicking dust between bare toes,
gathering dust in worn shoes,
carrying dust on thirsty tongues
breaking backs to break crops
to break cycles.

They walked the rows
to save the land.
They walked the rows
always racing the sun,
but falling behind every day
breaking backs to break crops
to break cycles.

They walked the rows
honoring all life,
but struggling for a living.
They walked the rows
gathering and losing the rows
before the children could walk
their own rows to break.
